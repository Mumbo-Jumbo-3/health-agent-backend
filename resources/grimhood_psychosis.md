Vitamin C can be useful particularly in a high cortisol or histamine state as it will regulate both factors, as well as mitigate oxidative stress. However, vitamin B6 deficiency is common in any GABA deficient conditions and will contribute to oxalate formation (kidney stones) from high-dose vitamin C supplementation. Personally, I would at least try to aim for 150-200mg in your diet and can include either whole food vitamin C (acerola cherry, camu camu, Kakadu plum), ascorbate salts, or ascorbic acid to hit 500-1000mg/day. TID stands for three times per day, so you can try to split it up similarly to his recommendation.
