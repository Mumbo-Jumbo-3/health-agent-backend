# Bone, Tissue & Surgery Repair Protocol

This really does apply to any and all injury types as the pathways involved are all very similar, though additional nuanced context will be noted.

Compliance is the distance between long-term bone pain suffering (algesia) and regenerating the whole body. Don't bother following this protocol if you are unwilling to remain compliant in your routine, as you will inevitably join the ranks of those who believe "holistic/herbal medicine doesn't work". Some people really do want to heal and care enough to do what's necessary and others don't care enough.

## Surgery Considerations

In the case of surgery for severe injury, in most cases, you'd rather refuse to fuse your bones together and would optimally opt for steel rods to hold position and keep discs or anything separated, this would be far more acceptable with long-term recovery and functionality in mind. Rods in ankles, for example, are much different than rods in the spine so this really is context-dependent. Too often people believe doctors are flawless and they will refuse to remove the rods because they have no faith in complete bone recovery or how to achieve it.

## Dietary Framework

Implementing the zero-tolerance dietary framework becomes crucial here to 1) avoid excessive inflammation that will inhibit the healing rate and 2) provide the micronutrients, amino acids, and fats for the body to recover itself effectively. You'll want to aim for 1-2g/lb of body weight for your protein intake, REAL broth made with tendons, beef ankle bones, chicken feet/meat or seafood stock, seafood and shellfish, organs such as liver and heart, eggs, red/game meats, quality poultry, and boar/pork, etc. LOTS of dairy/kefir and other calcium-rich foods, powdered egg shells, pearl powder, bone meal powder, and oyster shell powder work. Quality fruits, tubers, and green vegetables, preferably as close to fresh as possible are fine to ensure you're hitting your bases for micronutrients. If you're not eating organic or local, the Glyphosate Cleanse Protocol with glycine replacement to rebuild would be useful here as well, and may still even be useful regardless of dietary quality. (Glyphosate Cleanse Protocol will be written up after the Psychosis Protocol). Magnesium glycinate would make a useful addition along with Magnesium chloride (will mention more below). Glycine is huge for tissue repair, as is Lysine for cell wall membranes, and Inositol (Myoinositol/IP3 and Inositol hexaphosphate/IP6) for the ATP Phosphorus, gene expression, mitochondrial energy, and cell wall membranes.

## Herbal Broth Protocol

Black seeds and/or oil, Drynaria roosii/fortuneii, Astragalus, Rehmannia, Lion's mane fruiting body and mycelium extract, an ounce of oat straw, turmeric, and the rest of the herbs listed below. Throw it all into a tasty broth cooked 24-48 hrs on low. Add MgCl and IP3 + IP6 oral, topical MgCl, and BSO when it heals up a bit if bare sensitive flesh or wound is still exposed.

## Topical Magnesium Chloride

Applying loads of topical magnesium chloride before and after surgery is crucial, as the physiological stress of surgery, bone resetting, stitching, etc. will cause a resulting depletion of magnesium, plus it benefits with healing/repair rate, energy production, and so on and so forth. I will warn that care should be taken in the after-context of getting it on incision/stitches as it will sting as ocean water does initially, despite vastly accelerating wound healing. If you can handle the sting, go for it, but ensure the water you use in your solution is DISTILLED. No other water is acceptable due to infection risk. Oral intake is important as well, to bowel tolerance.

## MSM (Methylsulfonylmethane)

Methylsulfonylmethane (MSM) combined with the topical magnesium water would be excellent, as a source of sulfate, to direct repair nutrients, improve magnesium penetration deeply into the problem area, and as an analgesic in itself. Sulfate is especially useful to lubricate joints.

## Boron

Boron can be helpful here as well in bone repair and injury recovery in general, as well as aiding in regulating immune system inflammatory cytokines. Pure Borax (source of boron) in your topical magnesium chloride solution could be helpful namely if the incision has closed, though it does have anti-microbial properties in itself.

With enough magnesium, you may decide you don't need surgery, but that is ultimately up to the individual and does not constitute medical advice (context-dependent).

## Primary Herb: Drynaria (Gu-Sui-Bu)

The primary herb to this protocol is Drynaria roosii/Drynaria fortunei, also known as Gu-Sui-Bu which translates to Repairs Shattered Bones. In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), it is prescribed for traumatic injury, but more specifically bone and soft tissue injuries. It's also a highly effective analgesic, especially if paired with synergistic herbs and a proper diet. The powder form is best, tastes kind of like chocolate to me. It's very food-like and you could kind of treat it like a food ingredient, but in TCM, the average recommended dosage is 10-15 grams. Start low and build up slowly if you feel more comfortable doing so, but don't let it dissuade you at lower doses. But for therapeutic purposes, one wants to find the lowest effective dose because it has such opiate-like analgesic relief that you won't feel the pain from broken bones or tissue injury. As such, the big warning is while you may be pain-free, your fracture or injury is not healed yet and so do not strain whatever is broken/injured or you will reinjure it.

## Rehabilitation for Older Injuries

Rehabilitation for older injuries requires physical activity. Not too heavy, but enough to stimulate growth/repair and to stimulate blood flow to the area to carry micronutrients and electrons where they need to go. Dry brushing or something similar may also be helpful, as well as sunlight or UVB/UVA and red/IR light to do the same. I HIGHLY recommend finding a competent physical therapist or something similar in your area for one-on-one physical contact because they may be able to perceive a key factor that you are unable to without that baseline knowledge.
